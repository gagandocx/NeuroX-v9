# Choppy market filters and dual-mode regime system

NeuroX v9.20 adds four defenses against trading noise: an ATR floor that blocks entries when volatility drops below $0.50, a tick consistency check requiring 60%+ directional agreement in recent ticks, signal persistence demanding two consecutive same-direction signals before firing, and a dual-mode regime detector that switches from momentum to mean reversion when both the ATR ratio and a variance ratio test agree the market is ranging. The implementation threads these filters into the existing main loop at both the tick-momentum (pre-bar) and bar-momentum paths, and routes ranging regimes to a new mean reversion strategy that fades extremes of the local range.

**Watch for:** ATR filter absent from the tick-momentum path (confirmed) creates an inconsistency where the fastest entry path bypasses volatility gating. Signal persistence uses mutable global state that is shared between tick-mode and bar-mode execution, meaning a tick-momentum signal increments the counter that bar-momentum then evaluates (likely unintended cross-contamination). The tick consistency filter can block mean reversion entries in ranging markets even though the ranging regime *expects* noisy, directionless ticks (confirmed design tension).

## High-level view

The ATR filter gates bar-based momentum entries but is deliberately skipped for mean reversion (low ATR is the defining characteristic of ranging markets). However, the tick-momentum path (used before enough M1 bars form) applies tick consistency and signal persistence but never checks ATR, creating an unfiltered fast path that the bar path carefully guards.

Signal persistence relies on module-level globals (`signal_persistence_count`, `signal_persistence_direction`) mutated by every call regardless of which execution path triggered it. Because the main loop interleaves tick-mode and bar-mode checks within the same iteration, a tick signal and a bar signal within the same cycle will share and mutate the same counter.

Tick consistency compares directional moves over the last 40 ticks regardless of whether the system is in trending or ranging mode. In a ranging market, ticks naturally oscillate, so this filter may suppress the very mean-reversion signals the regime detector enables — a potential deadlock where the system declares "ranging" but then refuses to act on it.

## ATR filter asymmetry between execution paths

The bar-based trending path checks `passes_atr_filter(atr)` before firing, ensuring low-volatility conditions suppress momentum entries. The tick-momentum path (active before MIN_BARS_FOR_MOMENTUM bars are collected) applies `passes_tick_consistency` and `passes_signal_persistence` but never calls `passes_atr_filter`. This is the fastest entry path — it can fire within seconds of startup — and it's the one least protected against choppy conditions.

The omission may be intentional (tick mode has no computed ATR since bars haven't formed yet), but that means the system's first entries in a session will always bypass the volatility gate. If the EA starts up during a low-volatility Asian session, the tick path can fire signals that the bar path would later suppress.

A minimal fix: track a tick-level ATR proxy (e.g., max-min of recent N ticks) and gate on that, or defer all entries until at least one ATR computation succeeds.

## Signal persistence global state sharing

```python
# Module-level state
signal_persistence_count = 0
signal_persistence_direction = None
```

`passes_signal_persistence()` is called from three places:
1. Tick-momentum path (line 321)
2. Bar-momentum trending path (line 352)
3. Bar mean-reversion ranging path (line 362)

All three share the same counter. A sequence like: tick BUY (count=1), then bar momentum BUY (count=2, passes) can fire a bar signal "boosted" by a tick signal that measured a different lookback and threshold. Similarly, a momentum BUY followed by a mean-reversion BUY (same direction, different strategy logic) satisfies persistence despite the signals originating from contradictory regime assessments.

The persistence filter was designed to confirm directional conviction from repeated readings. Mixing readings from different signal generators dilutes that confirmation.

## Tick consistency vs. mean reversion tension

In the ranging path:

```python
if (mean_rev_direction != "FLAT"
        and can_trade()
        and has_capacity(bar_strength)
        and passes_hysteresis(mean_rev_direction, current_price)
        and passes_tick_consistency(tick_collector)
        and passes_signal_persistence(mean_rev_direction)):
```

`passes_tick_consistency()` requires 60% of recent ticks to move in the same direction. But a ranging market is characterized by ticks oscillating within a band — exactly the pattern that produces low consistency scores. The regime detector has just concluded the market is ranging (via ATR ratio + variance ratio), yet the entry filter then asks for directional tick agreement that a ranging market typically won't produce.

This creates a deadlock scenario: the regime detector says "mean revert", but the tick consistency filter says "too noisy to trade" — precisely because the market is ranging. Consider either skipping tick consistency for mean reversion entries (like ATR is skipped) or using a lower threshold for the ranging path.

## Mean reversion lookback mismatch

The regime is declared "ranging" based on 20 bars of history (`REGIME_VARIANCE_RATIO_LOOKBACK = 20`), but the mean reversion range is computed from only the last 10 bars (`MEAN_REVERSION_LOOKBACK = 10`). If the range extremes were established in bars 1-10 and the recent 10 bars have compressed into a tighter band, the $1.00 minimum range gate can suppress signals even though the wider regime window shows a tradeable range. This is a possible edge case — worth monitoring in live logs but not necessarily blocking.

## Test coverage gaps

Not tested: the main loop's filter ordering and short-circuit behavior under regime transitions. No test exercises the scenario where tick-mode signals contaminate bar-mode persistence state. No test verifies that the tick consistency filter doesn't systematically block mean reversion. The `check_position_timeout` reset of persistence state is tested, but re-entry behavior after timeout + regime change is not.
